Pani Puri

Pani Puri, also known as Golgappa or Puchka, is a popular street food snack from India. It consists of hollow, crispy puris (small, round, fried dough balls) that are filled with a mixture of spicy, tangy water (pani), mashed potatoes, chickpeas, chutney, and various spices.

  • Ingredients:

    • For the Puris:

  • 1 cup semolina (sooji/rava)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Water (as needed)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

    • For the Pani (Spicy Water):

  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves
  • 1-2 green chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 1-inch piece ginger
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind pulp
  • 1 tablespoon chaat masala
  • 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon black salt
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups chilled water

    • For the Filling:

  • cup boiled and mashed potatoes
  • 1/2 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves
  • Chaat masala to taste
  • Salt to taste

    Instructions:

    For the Puris:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine semolina, all-purpose flour, and salt. Gradually add water and knead into a stiff dough. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  2. After resting, knead the dough again for a couple of minutes. Divide it into small portions and roll them into small, thin circles (approximately 2-3 inches in diameter).
  3. Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadhai over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully slide in a few rolled puris at a time. Fry them until they puff up and turn golden brown. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on a kitchen towel. Repeat with the remaining puris.

    For the Pani (Spicy Water):

  1. In a blender or food processor, blend together mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chilies, and ginger to a smooth paste, adding a little water if needed.
  2. Strain the paste through a fine sieve to get a smooth mixture. Discard any solids remaining in the sieve.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the strained green paste, tamarind pulp, chaat masala, roasted cumin powder, black salt, and regular salt. Mix well.
  4. Add chilled water to the mixture and stir until everything is well combined. Adjust the spices and salt according to your taste preference. Refrigerate the pani until ready to serve.

    For the Filling:

  1. In a bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, boiled chickpeas, chopped onions, chopped tomatoes, chopped coriander leaves, chaat masala, and salt. Mix well to combine all the ingredients.
    2. To Assemble:
  2. Take a puri and gently crack it open from the top using your thumb and index finger to create a small hole.
  3. Fill the puri with a small spoonful of the prepared filling mixture.
  4. Dip the filled puri into the chilled pani and place it on a serving plate.
  5. Repeat the process with the remaining puris, filling, and pani.
  6. Serve immediately and enjoy the delightful and tangy Pani Puris.

    7. Note: You can adjust the spice levels of the pani by adding or reducing the amount of green chilies according to your taste preference.

Enjoy your delicious and flavorful Pani Puri!
